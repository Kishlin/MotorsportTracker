BelgianGP Race Pace - Trim the Wing, or Perish!

Jul 30, 2023

2 mins read

Spa is the track most suited to the current (large, quick, heavy, and powerful) F1 cars. The track layout presents 2 almost-full-throttle sectors (S1 and S3) and another (S2) made by medium and high-speed corners, so aerodynamic efficiency drives performance here. As the rest of the weekend was under the rain, teams had to gamble to find the correct setup: today's race, as expected, favoured those that opted for less loaded rear wings (Ferrari, Hamilton, among the top cars/drivers).

RB has found the 'holy grail' concerning aerodynamic setup: they have very low drag and excellent downforce. This allows them to run a less loaded lower plane and beam wings to gain top speed and efficiency while the downforce is retained through the more loaded upper plane. In qualifying and when overtaking, though, the DRS almost negates the drag penalty associated with the upper plane: this way, the car was competitive in all sectors and sessions.

Again, VER was much quicker than PER: +0.52s/lap pace advantage. HAM, though, was close behind: 0.17s/lap slower than PER, on average, and slightly quicker than Leclerc, too: LEC kept his position by starting further up on the grid.

Aston continues to underperform, but less so than in the previous races (at least considering Alonso'pace): the car was clearly quicker than the McLaren, which ran a compromised setup. In fact, the team opted for a very loaded rear wing and a general setup more geared towards rainy conditions: the bet didn't pay off, though, even though it allowed the team to get a solid result in the Sprint.

Ocon and Albon were very quick, too: the two teams ran very unloaded rear wings (SAR reached 358km/h during the race!), which made their performance in S1 and S3 excellent, and their tyre wear and cornering pace was decent. Haas, as in most seasons, worsened race after race and was the slowest team today.

Looking at the laptime evolution, we notice that VER was quickest in almost all the race segments. The only exceptions were laps 18-31 and in the last few laps, when HAM 'stole' the fastest lap from him. Laps fell around laps 17-26: Norris and Russel were quickest in those laps: the two had fresh tyres and a more loaded rear wing, making them shine in those conditions.

Concerning compounds, the soft tyre was best for around 10 laps, then the two converged as the Soft was affected by higher wear. Still, the Soft remained very competitive for 22 laps, which is over 30 laps of a 'normal' circuit considering how long a lap of Spa is!

The pits had no significant problems: the average pit time spanned from 22.89s to 24.37s; the field was very tight. RB, Ferrari and Mercedes were quick compared to Aston and McLaren: today's crown goes to AlphaTauri!
